Hi, I'm Xuening Xu

I am a fifth year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Stevens Institute of Technology, supervised by Prof. Xiaojiang Du. My research mainly focuses on security and safety of smart home IoT systems, discovering vulnerabilities and seeking solutions to enhance the protection of users and smart homes.

Prior to the Ph.D. study, I received my Master's degree in Computer & Information Sciences at Temple University and obtained my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics at University of Science and Technology of China.

Research
Projects

  • Discovering and Exploiting Vulnerability on Zigbee Devices
    • Discovered and demystified a vulnerability - Zigbee Hidden Attributes on widely-used Zigbee devices and implemented an end-to-end attack to exploit the vulnerability. Built an attack device using nRF5 SDK in C language on an nRF52840 DK.
    • Timely disclosed this vulnerability to device manufacturers, including Philips, Samsung, Amazon, Frient, Yale (lock manufacturer), Kwikset, and Connectivity Standards Alliance (CSA).
    • Received acknowledgements from Samsung, Amazon, and CSA. Furthermore, Amazon awarded a $2,500 bounty via HackerOne for the vulnerability disclosure. Check it out here!
    • The paper The Hidden Gems or Hidden Germs? Demystifying and Exploiting Zigbee Hidden Attributes has been submitted to one of the top-tier four security conferences.
  • Defensive System against the Delay Attacks in Smart Homes
    • Built an one-stop-for-all system in JavaScript in order to 1) connect and manage various IoT devices with different connectivities (e.g., Zigbee, Z-Wave, and Wi-Fi); 2) integrate with two popular IoT cloud platforms (i.e., Samsung SmartThings and IFTTT) with OAuth 2.0 implemented. A database was implemented using SQLite to store device status, as well as access tokens for interfacing with the cloud platforms.
    • Proposed an effective timeout-based approach to detect the delay attacks and then creatively used OpenVPN to handle the detected delay attacks.
    • The paper MP-Mediator: Detecting and Handling the Stealthy IoT Event and Command Delay Attacks has been accepted by RAID 2023.
  • Detection of Malicious Local Attacks on IoT Devices
    • Built an OpenWrt Wi-Fi router on a Raspberry Pi and adopted tcpdump to remotely capture network traffic for further analysis. Used Python scripts to analyze network layer information of the captured packets to detect malicious local attacks that exploit device local APIs, based on communication patterns between various parties in smart home IoT systems.
    • Simulated malicious local attacks by developing an iOS app to send malicious commands to the victim IoT devices, including plugs, light bulbs, lock, and vacuum.
    • The paper IoTtracer: Detecting Malicious Local Attacks on IoT Devices by Utilizing IoT System-level Traffic Patterns has been submitted to one of the top-tier four security conferences.
  • End-to-End Smart Speaker Protection System
    • Developed an Android app with Firebase Cloud Messaging integrated to measure the proximity of the user to the smart speaker without manual operation when the smart speaker is invoked, even if the smartphone is in the pocket all the time.
    • Built an end-to-end protection system in Python to first detect unauthorized voice commands using traffic analysis and then block them from being sent to the cloud servers for further processing.
    • The paper VoiceGuard: An Effective and Practical Approach for Detecting and Blocking Unauthorized Voice Commands to Smart Speakers has been published in DSN 2023.
  • Attack against Graph-based Classification System
    • Proposed a new attack by adding fake nodes to a graph without modifying existing edges to fool graph-based classification method. Formulated the proposed attack as an optimization problem and solved it using C++.
    • Evaluated the effectiveness of the proposed attack on the Facebook dataset and Enron dataset.
    • The paper Attacking Graph-Based Classification without Changing Existing Connections has been published in ACSAC 2020. [PDF]
  • Air Force UAV Authentication Schemes
    • Proposed two authentication schemes for two-way mutual authentication between the UAV and dumb sensors.
    • Implemented prototype systems in Python on Raspberry Pis to evaluate the proposed schemes.
    • The paper Effective UAV and Ground Sensor Authentication has been published in IEEE GLOBECOM 2019. [PDF]
